About

Organic farming represents a sustainable agricultural approach that prioritizes environmental stewardship, biodiversity conservation, and the well-being of farmers and consumers alike. By eschewing synthetic chemicals and genetically modified organisms (GMOs), organic farming promotes soil health, reduces pollution, and fosters resilience to climate change. Its emphasis on natural processes and biodiversity not only produces nutritious food but also supports local economies and enhances food security. Embracing organic farming promises a healthier future by safeguarding ecosystems, improving public health, and ensuring sustainable food production for generations to come.

Organic Farming

Organic farming, also known as ecological farming or biological farming, is an agricultural system that utilizes organic-originated fertilizers and emphasizes techniques such as crop rotation and companion planting. The methods used in organic farming are designed to minimize the use of non-organic substances.
- Uses biological fertilizers derived from animal and plant wastes and nitrogen-fixing cover crops
- Uses pest controls that are ecologically based
- Uses natural pesticides such as pyrethrins
- Prohibits or limits synthetic substances
- Prohibits genetically modified organisms, nanomaterials, sewage sludge and plant growth regulators
- Encourages mixed cropping and biological pest control
- Encourages the fostering of insect predators
