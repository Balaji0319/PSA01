FAQ
    1.What is organic farming?

    A.Organic farming is a method of agriculture that avoids using synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and genetically modified organisms (GMOs) to grow crops and raise animals. .

    2.What are the benefits of organic farming?

    A.Organic farming promotes soil health, biodiversity, and ecosystem balance, and reduces environmental pollution and health risks.

    3.What is the difference between organic and conventional farming?

    A.Organic farming uses natural methods and materials to control pests and diseases, while conventional farming uses synthetic chemicals.

    4.Are organic farms more productive than conventional farms?

    A.Organic farms can be as productive as conventional farms, but may require more labor and diverse crop rotations.

    5.How do organic farmers control pests and diseases?

    A.Organic farmers use natural methods like crop rotation, biological pest control, and cultural practices to manage pests and diseases.
